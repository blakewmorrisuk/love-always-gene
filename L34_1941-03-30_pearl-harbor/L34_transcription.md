                                                          U.S.S. New Orleans
                                                          Pearl Harbor,
                                                          Honolulu, T.H.
                                                          March 30, 1940
                                                          F Division

I.

Dearest Joan,

I was so glad to hear from you agin and it seems as if your letters are so far between. Joan I sorry that I haven't been writting often but I thought I had been writting you a letter once a week. Guess though I forgotten too write one week although I thought I had.

Well Joan you asked what I have been doing lately. There are about the same old routine every day. The most I do every day is running a motor boat. I finally work myself up to coxwain of it. It a small boat about thirty feet long and will haul 26 men at one time.

Glad to hear that the weather has warmed up and hope it will stay that way. Yes the weather out here is still hot and the shorts that you asked about are comming along fine. Hope you passed all the tests alright, which I know you did.

So, Carroll, Tom and Martha is comming or has been home by now. Wished I could have been

II.

there also but guess I as soon wished for a fortune for as much good it would do me. Anyway it can't be as long as it has been.

That girl at Highland must think a lot of Carroll. Carroll doesn't write to me anymore and I don't care much anyway.

Joan I'm getting the Stanford paper now and I saw the big write up about the new theater that they have in Stanford now.

How is Raymond getting along? I hope that darn lamb dies which it probably will with a name like that.

That Mr. Hampton must be a pretty nice man I think. I never forget the day I met him when I was getting off the train there at Stanford. Of course I didn't know who he was and he kindly made me a little sore in the first place asking so many questions about me. It was wonder I hadn't told him where to get off at which probably wouldn't been so good after all.

Well, Joan we go back out to sea tomorrow but only stay out until Friday. How would you like to be out here in

III.

this land of sunshine?

Joan I don't know anything worth writting. By the way you never did tell me when your birthday is. I know its some time in May.

Jo, I get lonesome, as you say lots of times myself. Hope my letters cheers you up some. You needn't worry about me forgetting too think about you. [[Sometime I wish to hell I could forget that I ever saw you.]]

Will have to bring this letter to a close now. Tell every one I say, hello for me. Always looking for a letter with your handwriting on it.

                                                          With all kinds of Love
                                                                          Yours, "Gene"
                                                                                  Raymond
