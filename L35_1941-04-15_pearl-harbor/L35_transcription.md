April 12, 1941
                                                          U.S.S. New Orleans
                                                          Pearl Harbor, Honolulu, T.H.
                                                          F Division

Dearest Joan,

Received your letter a few days ago and will try to write you a few tonight while thinking of you. Although guess you think I have forgotten all about you for not answering sooner. Well I haven't even though I don't write.

We are in port today and have been in for about a week. We having a little repair work done getting it in [[better shape for war. Its only around the corner now]] which I guess you know.

Joan, I hope your mother is over the trouble she has with her shoulder. I also glad to hear that you was alright and I know you not mean although you might think you are. You are a darn nice girl, Joan if I have to tell em myself and I hope you will stay that way. Anyway you probably better off with me not being around. [strikethrough: what you think?]

Guess your school is about out by

(2.)

now. I bet you will really be glad too.

I got letter from home a few days ago and they said, Carroll, Thomas and Martha were home for the week end. Joan you asked if I get homesick. Well I don't think so. If a guy thought much about home way [strikethrough: down] here he would go crazy. Its just a little over five thousands miles from home. So you got to try and not think to much about home.

We will or anyway they say we will go back to the West Coast last of next month. I might get a leave and agin I might not. [[If war does break loose I won't see you until it is over, that is if I am still kicking.]]

Well what news around there Jo? Everything quite and fine I bet. You said spring had come and Sweetheart let me tell you it's really getting hot down here in this country. What are you going to do this summer? I'm going to send you something before long

(3.)

that you might enjoy having. I hope so anyway.

Well Joan guess I will have to bring this letter to a close. There isn't very much that I know to write and you will have too excuse this writting for I trying to finish before the movie begins. Be a good girl and save one kiss for me. You the only girl I ever thought anything about and sometime I hate myself for that.

                                                          Always,
                                                          Eddie—"Gene"
