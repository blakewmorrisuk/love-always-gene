Received your letter yesterday and as I think we are sending a plane into Pearl Harbor with the mail tomorrow I will try to write you a few lines tonight while thinking of you. Guess you think I write to darn much anyway. If I do don't bother to answer them for I know how it would be. I write them because you are the only one that I think is worth writting them to. So if you are just writting to make me feel better just forget all about them. Anyway I may not be worth it. What you think?

Well, Joan I will tell you before I start that there isn't any news to write. We were in the Harbor for four days and came right back out to sea agin for ten more days. You could see that I couldn't write anything but how lovely the ocean (over)

(2.)

were and maybe you wouldn't care to hear it anyhow.

Glad to hear that your high school basketball team are doing good this year. It wouldn't do for them to win the State Champion and if something [strikethrough] happen that they did I know they never would be able to find hats big enough for their heads. I got a letter from Dad yesterday also and he were telling me about the games. Said Earl had went a time or two to see the games. I think Earl is liking Highland school better now. He sure better not do like I did. Stop before he finished high school. I'm in a pretty place to be now but I guess I can take it.

[[I will give the United States five months before we will be right in the middle of war.]]

Yes, Joan I know about Mrs. Holyclaw going to Arizona for her health. When we came out to the coast we came right through the middle of Arizona. I sure didn't think much of the state myself, but guess it's the right

(3.)

for what she got.

Glad to hear about you getting along with the sheep so good. Keep the good work up, Joan and you will wished you had something real soft to sit on one of these days. I always did say keep hands off of things that doesn't belong to you. (If you know what I mean.) Don't listen to me, Joan, go ahead and play with the sheep. Sometime I think I'm crazy myself. In fact I can't figure out what I doing at times myself. So who am I to tell you what to do.

Well Darling, I don't know very much more to write. I guess when you get in that shape you gotta do something and I can't think of anything else to do but bring this [strikethrough] letter to a close with a sweet kiss.

Write if you really find time enough Joan. So I'm all yours until next time.
