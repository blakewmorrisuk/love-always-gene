  A letter to Gene from Don Chapin, eighteen months after the previous letter in the archive. Gene was no longer aboard the New Orleans, no longer in the Pacific, and no longer in the Navy: the envelope is addressed to 921 Cramer Avenue, Apartment 2, Lexington, Kentucky. Don was now a Coxswain aboard the destroyer U.S.S. Rowe in the western Pacific. He had written to confront Gene about a letter Gene had sent to Don's wife Betty that had upset her. Well Gene I guess it's about time that I drop you a few lines. Should have done so sooner but have been sort of busy you know.

How have you been? I hope that you are getting along alright because I can imagine how it is not to be feeling so good.

[[Received a letter from my wife the other day and she had enclosed the letter you had written to her. Well I guess you know what kind of a spot that puts me in. I've tried to think it over Gene and from the sound]] of thing I'd say that you have lost your faith lately. I bet you kid she is my wife and I would be human if I said that was the kind of a letter I wanted a buddy of mine to write her. It was here in radio's and I let you live & nothing but I think you were in the wrong did not telling me that it was in no shape to ship so you let it stay in the hospital. I know I wouldn't have and I'm sure O.K. wouldn't have thought no more about it.

As for buying for it you know darn well that I wouldn't let you do anything of the kind. I know your letter upset her, Gene and I feel bad about the whole works. I don't know if you know it or not but she has been and still is a pretty sick girl and I worry about her an awfully lot. Between that & this damn war I'm in a sweat all the time. The longer I stay out here the more discouraged I get. It's still rough and will not likely get rougher.

I haven't heard nothing about Jack but I'm still hoping to run across him. Have had a little dope on our friends but I can't tell you what it is as yet.

Right now I'm on the M.A.A. force and all I can say about that is that it's an ungrateful job. I still haven't gotten like Maudsley yet anyway.

Well it's almost midnight so I had better close. Hope to hear from you Gene and give my regards to Joan. Oh yes as one favor to me try not think to hard of B.S. Maybe someday we can all meet as straighten things out right. [[Good luck kid.]]
