Thursday Oct 23
                                                          U.S.S. New Orleans
                                                          % Postmaster
                                                          Bremerton, Wash.
                                                          F Division

Dearest Joan;

Received your letter today and will write a few lines tonight. Glad you are fine but there surely something wrong with you. You said guess the letter wouldn't reach me before I left as I were leaving the 25th of October and it was the 21st of that day. Well I got the letter today & it's only the 23rd of October. Joan, time sure is flying or you or school lessons are getting you upset one. [[Guess you heard the report over the radio about the U.S.S. New Orleans, being lost for two days in a heavy fog at sea.]] Well, anyway we got back to the navy yard this afternoon. We went out on a testing run but while we were out an heavy fog set in & had to stay out. We just dropped the Anchor and had to stay there for two days until the fog lifted before we could see to come in.

So, Joan you want me to wait until the day you finish—ed nurses training then ask you to marry me then. I think you taking a lot for granted. Why don't you first stop & think how I would feel if you were in my place. Joan I wouldn't ask you to marry me then if you were the only girl in this world tonight. If you went to all that trouble for a nursing career I wouldn't have the nerve to spoil it. I know you haven't much confidence in me & I can see your point. Yes, I'm in Navy now & by the time I got out it probably be tough going outside, but it's to late for me to cry about that. You wrong I think Joan I'm not the one for you. It's some one else much higher then I am. If nursing what you want it's all yours for my part & I be a fool to look at it any other way. Joan, I not at all mad at you & never will be for that matter. I have been just (over) crazy fool to have falling in love with you so much. Jack Parkhurst & myself were putting in a request to be transferred to the East coast & we probably would get it. We could come home quite often around there at least four or five times a year. I not even going to try any more and just stay where I'm at. When I get out of the navy I be home for a few days anyway and not until then. It's the best way Joan & no use kidding myself. You been doing everything in the world (I said or really mean about everything) to lead me on for the last two years. Joan, you might love me alright but it's not the way I love you. Joan you are a sweet girl & I would do anything in this world for you, but it's just impossible for me to ask you to marry me then. If it's alright with you just forget that I ever ask you for that is the best thing to do. Don't get mad Joan for you know I have let you have your way every since I been going with you, but this is one time I drawing a line between it. So from now on I'm letting nature take it's course.

Well Joan there isn't anything new around here to write that would interest you. This time next week guess we will be on our way to California. It's really beginning to get a little cold around here, but I like it. Although good old California sunshine will feel awful good agin. From there on to the Island where the mountains meets the moon of Hawaii. Joan we will probably won't leave until next Thursday Oct 30 as the fog delayed us for a couple of days. [[Here is what I going to do — Wednesday night Oct 29 I going to call you on the telephone long distance. I want you to go down to Mother to be up there for I want to say a few words to her also before I leave. It's only an three minute talk.]] (continue back of page 6)

                                                          "Gene"

P.S. The call will be about or between 9:00 & 10:30 O'clock Wednesday nite by your time. If you changed your mind about the promise Joan let me for an final answer then. So until then,
                                                          Yours
                                                              Gene

(Be sure to have Mother up to your house then.)
