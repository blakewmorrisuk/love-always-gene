July 2, 1940
U.S. Naval Training St.
Out Going Unit
Great Lakes, Ill.

Dear Joan,

Will write a line tonight to say I made the trip fine. I got in the station around 12 O'clock today and I couldn't say I enjoyed the trip very much. I didn't sleep any on the train coming up and you know how I fell tonight. Joan, I am so home-sick I can't hardly eat anything.

You don't know how bad I hated leaving. I guess it would have been the best if I hadn't come home. Most of the other boys feel the same way. If you say anything to them they almost cry. They know it'll be a long time before they can go home again.

Joan, most of my old company 26 had gone to sea. The rest of them are leaving tonight. If I had come in yesterday, I would have been leaving with them. I think I go out next Tuesday anyway; I'm going to go on mess cooking in the morning so I can get on the ship I want.

Joan, I am looking for that picture every day so be sure and send it as quick as possible.

Will have to close now. Be sure and answer right back for I am not going to let you stop writing, if I have to write you every day.

Love,

Gene

P.S. Joan, I'm sorry the way I treated you Sunday night. I'm not at all and mad and I hope you are not. Be good, I am really going to miss you.
