May 30, 1940
U.S. Naval Training St.
Company 28
Great Lakes, Illinois

Dear Joan,

Received your letter a day or two ago and will try and answer this afternoon while not doing anything else.

Well, I'm out of the hospital now and in Company 28 instead of co. 26. I only lost one week of training while I was there.  Today is a holiday for the Navy and all we had to do was to march in a parade this morning. It was really nice, for every company in the station were in it. There were also a bunch of visitors. They had planes to fly over the lake and drop flowers (and so on).

Well, we have been on the rifle range this week to see how good a shot we are. Only have one more day left of that. We will have it tomorrow. Our shoulders are getting awful sore. There was a boy that got his hand about blown off the other day. The gun he was shooting jammed some way on him and well, it just blowed up.

We graduate June 19 and I will be home June 20 for 13 days before going aboard ship. It'll probably be a long time before I get another, way the war sounds now. Looks like it's going to take the United States to stop it. They are really preparing for it, too. You only have to sign up in the Navy for four years now and they're calling them in fast. They also have only five weeks of training (looks like I signed up too soon).

Joan, your stationary is really nice looking, and you can write good letters when you want to.

How are all the family; fine I hope. Are you still housecleaning? Don't work too hard.

Things are green and pretty around here now. Although, it's still a little cool most of the days. I guess it's caused by the lakes.

I'm about out of news to write. Anyway (of) things that you care to hear. (Or would you?). I bet not.

Did I write out of time the other time? I'm sorry if I did.

Write to me soon as possible if you feel like it.

Your Friend,

Eugene L.

P.S. Don't forget Company 28
