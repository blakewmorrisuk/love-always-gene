  The first letter after the attack. Four days after Japanese planes came in over the harbor, Gene wrote a single page of plain paper, no letterhead, no date, no closing endearment. He signed it Raymond E. Lankford, his full legal name, because every letter he sent from now on would pass through a naval censor and a sailor's mail had to read as official. The envelope is the first in the archive to carry the round PASSED BY NAVAL CENSOR stamp. 'We aren't suppose to write a thing about whats going on.' The same day this letter was mailed, Germany and Italy declared war on the United States.

Just a few words to say hello & let you know that I'm fine. Received your two letters a few days ago & you will have to take this for an answer. [[We aren't suppose to write a thing about whats going on.]] Guess that won't be hard to understand. Write to me soon & I will try too agin soon. [[I know how every one feels about this back there. Anyway there isn't any use in worrying.]]

Give my love to all.
