  **THIS IS A SOUVENIR POSTCARD FOLDER, not a letter.** A folded illustrated tourist booklet from El Paso, Texas, sent by Gene to Joan in July 1940 — sent at the 1½¢ "postage without message" rate, meaning no handwritten content was added (this is reflected by the preprinted "POSTAGE 1½¢ WITHOUT MESSAGE" indicator on the front).

  The folder contains an accordion-fold panel of color-printed views of El Paso landmarks (Mills Street, San Jacinto Plaza, Hotel Paso del Norte, Bassett Tower, Mount Franklin, the Rio Grande, etc.) plus a printed boilerplate description of El Paso on the back cover. The folder unfolds into a long panoramic strip showing the city's scenes.

  Postmark on the front: "EL PASO, TEX JUL 11 7:30 PM 1940."

  Address handwritten on the right half of the front (in Gene's hand): "Miss Joan Northcutt / Stanford, Ky. / Route 9" (or possibly Route 4 — last digit unclear). No return address. No stamp (1½¢ rate paid via the preprinted franking).

  Inside date: NONE — souvenir folder has no message space.

  Salutation: NONE.

  Signature: NONE.

  **Historical context:** July 11, 1940 — Gene was likely transiting through El Paso en route to San Diego (where the U.S.S. New Orleans was based before moving to Pearl Harbor in mid-1940). The postcard predates the L25 letter (January 2, 1941) in calendar time and is the EARLIEST item in the archive other than the other 1940 letters L104/L105/L106. The folder is filed at the end of the queue because Blake's original numbering followed photo-album page order, not chronological order. Chronologically L103 is the FIRST item in the Lankford-Northcutt archive that has been transcribed (predates the L25 NEW YEAR letter by six months).

  Notable content: (1) **First documented item from Gene to Joan in the archive** — Gene sent Joan a tourist postcard folder from El Paso during transit. The simple act of mailing-without-writing — "thinking of you, no time to say more" — is the earliest tangible Gene-to-Joan token in the corpus.
