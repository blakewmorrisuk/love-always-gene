  Two days after receiving his orders to leave Great Lakes, Gene was on a train across the southern desert toward San Diego. From the El Paso station he mailed Joan a tourist folder of color photographs of the city, sent at the cheapest postal rate, no message allowed. It is the earliest thing he ever sent her. He had been in the Navy three months and was a day or two short of reporting to the U.S.S. New Orleans for the first time.

[[Address: Miss Joan Northcutt / Stanford, Ky. / Route 9 (or 4)]]
Postmark: El Paso, Tex. — Jul 11, 7:30 PM, 1940
Sent at 1½¢ "postage without message" rate

Printed material only — the souvenir folder contains color-printed views of El Paso landmarks (Mills Street, San Jacinto Plaza, Hotel Paso del Norte, Bassett Tower, Mount Franklin, the Rio Grande Bridge, etc.) and a printed boilerplate description of El Paso on the back cover.

No salutation, no body, no signature — only Gene's hand on the address.
