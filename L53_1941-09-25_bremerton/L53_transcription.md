Your letter came a few minutes ago and thought I would answer it right back. Let me tell you right now, sweetheart that you can write an awful sweet letter. I'm sorry about Joan getting sick on cherry preserves. Next time I guess you won't eat so many of them.

I have had a little cold for the last two days. It isn't bed though. This is an awful easy place to catch a cold. Just got to get use to the climate I guess.

I hated that about Mrs Gentry, for she was such a nice woman. That family will have a hard time without her. (over) I got an letter from Martha yesterday and said that she had been sick, but had gotten better. I believe she works study to hard, myself. She shouldn't work that hard for in the long run it isn't worth it. Guess, though you have it still in your head to be an nurse. Maybe it would be the best I don't know. I thought it would be the best for me to join the navy, but now I can see what a great mistake I made after it's to late. So you can see I'm no one to judge things like that. (Joan this darn pen still doesn't write. You know I never did have one that was worth two cents (could be, that it's not the pens fought.)

Joan, you asked if I still like to have that promise. You know darn well that still want it. There isn't anything that would be more satisfying then that. My eyes would be sparkling with joy if you would. [[Think it over Honey and let me know.]] I know that I love you more then anything in this world and I believe you love me too. I don't know what to think about this dam war. [[Sometimes I think that it won't last long and again it looks like it might last for years.]]

Joan, you never said how the place on your breast was. Did you ever go to the Dr. with it? I'm glad your grandma has forgiven us for the trouble we cause her. I saw in the Stanford paper where she was feeling somewhat better.

Joan, I saw an awful good movie last night and it made twice I have seen it but I still thought it was awful good. If it comes to Stanford go & see it. "So Ends our Night" is the name of it. There is a good on also tonight and I going to see it. It's "Shepherd of the Hills."

Well, Darling, please write to me more often for your letters seem so far apart. I'm not much of an writter but I will promise that I answer every one of them. Be good Joan and I will be thinking about you every minute of the day. I have been dreaming about you every night since I been back.
