Wed. Sept 17
                                                          U.S.S. New Orleans
                                                          % Postmaster
                                                          Bremerton, Wash.
                                                          F Division

1.

Dearest "Joan",

Your letter came today and let me tell you I was glad to see your hand writting agin. Really, Joan you can write the best letter than anyone I know. I had just about giving up hopes of getting a letter from anybody until I got yours today. I can't or haven't yet had heard a word from home. Hope they aren't all dead down there for I have been gone from home now about two weeks and I don't see why I shouldn't have heard from some of them by now. It not because of me either because I wrote to them at least four times. I know though, that (over)

(2)

I can always depend on getting a nice letter from you and I love you so much for it that there isn't enough words to say it in.

Joan I'm so glad that I didn't cause any hard fillings between us while I was at home and I know if I had it was all on my fault. You might understand how it was on my part. See, knowing that I was there for only a few days and had to come back to the navy. It's not like going home to stay as long a guy wants too for in the navy he knows there's a time set when he got to be back. Guess it's just kindly got my nerves all upset when I thought leaving agin and not knowing when I would get to come back.

(3)

I'm glad, Joan you like that telephone call from Chicago for I really had to hear you talking agin before I left for Seattle. You might get a long distant call from me any time and anywhere. While we are in the yards here I can call you right from the ship. The other day someone rush down and said I had a long distant telephone call and I like to have went through the deck for I thought that some thing was wrong at home. Well it turned out to be Jack Parkhurst calling me. He has been gone now for about a week on his leave and I bet he never get back to this ship.

Joan, I found out that [[unless we are in war before we leave the yards that we will go right back out to Honolulu agin]]. I sure hate to even think about it (over)

(4)

that is so far away from home. To be this far away is bad enough and that's over six thousand miles from you. Guess, you are right about me not having much of a chance getting out of the navy now. It isn't going be long now before we are going to be in the war. That isn't a very nice thing to be in for [[you can be living today and dead that night]]. You can never tell, though for we might go through the whole war without firing a shot and agin we could be sunk the first week. It's not a very good thing to think about anyhow.

Joan, I'm awful glad you are gaining weight and also please to hear about that place

(5)

going away you had on your breast. You don't want to let that five subjects you are taking in school get the best of you. Just think you will be out of school before long. The two years will [strikethrough: pass] past quit. Tell Charolette thanks a lot for naming that baby brother of hers after me.

Well Darling don't you think I wrote about enough for once. I know you are tired of reading this stuff. Anyway I will be writting agin soon. You said that you would answer every letter I wrote so I'm going to hold you to that promise. You know darn well how crazy in love I am with you and you will be always [strikethrough: of] the only one from the bottom of my heart. Be good, honey and write to me soon.

                                                          With Lots of love & kisses
                                                                          Always yours
                                                                                  "Gene"
(over)
