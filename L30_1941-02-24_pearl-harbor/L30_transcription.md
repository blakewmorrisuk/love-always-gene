  Writing from sea. The mail was being flown off the ship by plane every few days. Gene gave his firmest stateside-return projection so far, three months out, and then walked into the harder territory of trying to ask Joan, directly, whether she actually cared about him. Received your sweet letter about two days ago and thought I would write you a few lines today. The reason I haven't answer sooner is that we out to sea this week and haven't had very much time to write. The paper is alright Joan for it doesn't make any difference to me what kind of paper you write on just so you will.

Hope this finds everyone OK and that big show gone by now. I am alright myself except a little blue today. Guess there isn't anyone that doesn't feel blue sometimes. (Am I right?) (over) Well Jo as I said we are riding the waves today and will go in about Thursday. So this letter may not be mailed until then for when we out at [strikethrough: to] sea they fly it in by plane and we just sent a plane in yesterday. [[I will be glad when they turn the nose of this ship to the states and it won't be more than three months before we will.]] These islands are alright for a while, but a guy gets sick of them after awhile.

Joan, I hope you passed that Latin alright and I believed you did. I bet that is a hard subject to learn but honey don't let it get you down.

I got a letter from Dad the same day I got yours. He doesn't write much to me. It was the second letter I received from him since I have been in the navy. They are looking for me to come home when we go back to the states. I hope I can but you never know when you get a leave. They say about everybody on this ship will but that doesn't mean anything. But if I do get a few days off I'll be there. Would you care whether I did or not? Sometimes I think you like me and again you just seem like you don't care at all. You know you never have told me whether you did or not. Remember the night you got a little mad and said that you hated me. The last night when I were home on leave and you got so mad because — ? Still know what you said? Anyway Joan, I will never forget you as long as I'll live. If you know how much I really love you, [strikethrough: you] you could understand how I feel. You know yourself I couldn't make you like me. Maybe I have written enough and better stop. What you say?

Well will close now for its getting about supper time. No, Joan I'm not Mess cooking and haven't been for about three months now.
