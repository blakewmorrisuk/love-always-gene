[V-MAIL FORM]
From: EUGENE LANKFORD / U.S.S. New Orleans / % Fleet Postmaster / San Francisco, Calif. / F Division
To: Miss Joan Northcutt / Stanford, Kentucky / Route 1
[V-Mail Control: NY 11[??]]
Sunday, Feb. 14, 1943

Dearest Joan;

Just a line tonight to say hello and let you know everything is fine with me. Sure hope this finds you & the family in the very best of health.

[[Joan, I bet it has been an awfully long time since I last wrote you a letter. I have received a lot of nice letters the past weeks, but still I couldn't bring myself to write again. Why? I got your very delightful picture several days ago.]] I think both of us is something that has happened back of in this. Maybe time and I didn't know what is it. We had started to get drove away to back at it really not much. So you saying you got there next first truth me of with how well we use to get along together. I want too tell you to think it over. As history just to see what some out & love what one and yet I don't think we will end up love. So you'll only better of my heart. I know the love we have for each other. [[Maybe after the war is over things [will] get back [together] on but is be only us.]] Don't planning on the everything home. Don't know yet but I tell you in time. Bye

                                                          [Gene]
