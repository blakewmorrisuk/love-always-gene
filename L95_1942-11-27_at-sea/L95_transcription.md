  **THE LAST LETTER GENE WROTE BEFORE THE BATTLE OF TASSAFARONGA.** Three days later, on the night of November 30, 1942, the U.S.S. New Orleans was hit by a Japanese Long Lance torpedo that detonated her forward magazines and blew off her bow forward of turret 2. **183 crewmen died, 24 missing.** Gene survived. This is the closing voice of pre-Tassafaronga Gene.

  Notable content: (1) **Gene's birthday + traditional sailor hazing** — "Had an birthday a few days ago and they say I suppose to be a man now. Some of the boys gave me quite a beating too. Should have kept my mouth shut I guess." Gene's birthday is in late November (L70 Nov 23 1941 mentioned a Martha birthday present; this confirms the timing). Birthday-hazing — the traditional shipboard ritual of "beating" or "spanking" the birthday boy — celebrated three days before the torpedo. The crew was still doing this two days before they would face the worst night of their lives.

  (2) **"I'm comming back to all of that one of these days … Just keep your fingers crossed"** — "I'm comming back to all of that one of these days. Will make up for all of this sooner or later. Just keep your fingers crossed." Gene's closing affirmation paired with a wartime superstition phrase. Written three days before the Tassafaronga torpedo, the "fingers crossed" reads with terrible weight in hindsight.

  Also: **JOAN SENT A POCKET BOOK DESPITE GENE'S REQUEST NOT TO** — "Oh yes, I did get the pocket book in the last mail and thanks a million. It's really a grand present and I'm crazy about it. You shouldn't have send it though. Remember I told you not to send me anything this year. Wished I was close enough to give you a spanking" (L94 had said no Christmas present); **Mail going to be hard to send/receive** — "Well Darling I haven't received any mail either and don't know when I will again" — Gene anticipating extended mail blackout; **Letter to home folks just finished**; **Cold weather memory** — "I can remember those cold nights we use to share together"; **"Will make up for all of this sooner or later"** — Gene's recurring promise to return.

As this is an opportunity for me to get a letter off the ships I'll take time out to write you a line. Just finished writing to the home folks too. I know or hope you are anxious to hear from me again as it has been quite sometime since I wrote last. Well Darling I haven't received any mail either and don't know when I will again. Oh yes, I did get the pocket book in the last mail and thanks a million. It's really a grand present and I'm crazy about it. You shouldn't have send it though. Remember I told you not to send me anything this year. Wished I was close enough to give you a spanking.

Well sweet heart sure hope you are fine. I'm alright as usual. [[Had an birthday a few days ago and they say I suppose to be a man now. Some of the boys gave me quite a beating too. Should have kept my mouth shut I guess.]]

Well, Darling what you doing these days? Working & studying I bet. Having plenty of cold weather, no doubt. Sure wished I was there to see a part of it. I can remember those cold nights we use to share together. [[I'm comming back to all of that one of these days. Will make up for all of this sooner or later. Just keep your fingers crossed.]]

Joan, got to make this letter short as the mail is leaving the ship soon. Write to me when ever you can. I'm writting when it's possible, so don't wait for my letters. Be good, Darling and I'm always thinking about you.
