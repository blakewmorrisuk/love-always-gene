May 17, 1940
U.S. N.T.S
Great Lakes, Ill.
Company 28

Dear Joan,

Your letter came safe and sound today. You are about the only one I can receive a letter from. They say I don't write often enough down home, but I be darn if I get a letter once a month from them.

Well, Joan I am through with my training and ready for Germany, now. We have taken all our examinations and have finished the drilling part. Well, we been loafing around for the last for four or five days. I will start home 9:30 Wednesday night and get to Stanford 11:00 the next morning. That's a long time to be on the road, but the trains going that way is awful slow.  They have to stop at so many small towns.

You said I was getting homesick. Well, I don't think so. It doesn't pay to think about home here. I let every day take care of itself.

Joan, what in the world is it you got to tell me anyway? Why couldn't you have told me in the letter.

Tell Peggy I hope her tooth has stopped hurting by now. You sure must be having a grand time (I hope).

I can't write a letter anyway, if I know anything to write. Don't look for me until you see me coming.

Your Pal.

Be Good,

Lankford

P.S. a "Boot Leave" is what they called the first leave that you get in the Navy when you have finished the training (catch on?) Excuse this mess (over).
