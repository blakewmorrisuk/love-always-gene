note: |
  A letter from Mary Parkhurst, Jack's wife, in Mount Vernon, Missouri, to Gene at his Lexington apartment. Gene had written asking for Jack's current address. From this letter we know two things for the first time in the archive: Gene had married Joan, and Gene had been discharged from the Navy. Mary's husband Jack was now a radar operator on the U.S.S. Massachusetts, somewhere in the Philippines, after nine weeks without a letter to her. He had once shot down a Mitsubishi bomber. 'I'm glad for you two that you are home,' Mary wrote. 'I know how very awful it is without my Johnny home.'

Yes I did recognize your name Johnny, I call Jack, Johnny — Johnny has mentioned you, & your picture is in Johnny's album of Hawaii several times. I was very much surprised to hear from you.

Yes I know you & Johnny were shipmates for a long time. Johnny is a radar-operator now — [[while in Pearl Harbor sometime ago Johnny sent a Firecontrol man from off the U.S.S. New Orleans & learned you were discharged & that Chapin has been transferred to another ship.]]

Johnny told me to send you & also Chapin our wedding announcements. I'll tell Johnny you married Joan — I knew you were engaged a long time ago — Johnny told me.

I write Johnny everyday & I'll tell him about Joan & hearing from you. Oh yes — I don't know the Firecontrol man's name that Johnny saw. He, Johnny saw our resident there at the same time he was there at Pearl Harbor.

Of course it isn't asking too much to get Johnny's address. I know he will be very glad to hear from you. His address —

                                                          Mr. John S. Parkhurst, "F" Division
                                                          U.S.S. Massachusetts
                                                          % Fleet Post Office
                                                          San Francisco, California

I know you do have a lot of news to tell him. I'll send your address to Johnny today. Yes I'll tell Johnny about Chapin (his picture is in Johnny's album too) & that Chapin has a baby.

Where Johnny is at present I can't say — but I think he is in the Philippines now.

It is going on 9 weeks since I heard from Johnny but I look for a letter everyday. We, Johnny & I were in Bremerton Wash. May 29 to July 10, this year. Then my Johnny had to go back but was suppose to get a transfer in not longer than 3 months from July. We hope he will get a transfer to Wash. D.C. to go to school anytime now. I'm hoping Johnny will be home for Christmas, not hearing lately I hope means Johnny is on his way home. Johnny is a radar operator Petty Officer 3rd Class now. Officer Riley (the division officer) said Johnny was the best operator on the ship. Johnny shot down a "Betty" Jap bomber too.

Yes I recognized who the picture was of. I would like to have it back if I'm sending it back to you. I have Johnny's album he brought home in 1941.

[[The New Orleans was due in Bremerton when I came home — the bow was blown off. I suppose you have read the book Chaplin Forgy wrote about the New Orleans. I saw Johnny's ship in, Bremerton. It's a beauty.]]

I hope sometime Johnny & I will meet you & Joan. I'm glad for you two that you are home. I know how very awful it is without my Johnny home.

I was glad to give you Johnny's address as I know he will be glad to hear from you. I hope you soon hear from him. The best of luck to you & Joan.
