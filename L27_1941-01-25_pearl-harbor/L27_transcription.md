Jan. 23, 1941
                                                          % U.S.S. New Orleans
                                                          Pearl Harbor, Honolulu, T.H.
                                                          F Division

Dearest Joan,

Received your letter at last and was glad you hadn't forgotten to write. You said in the letter that you wrote two more letters since Xmas. Well this is the first one I got. Guess the other two haven't had time yet. They probably got hold over some place or other and will be along later.

I also got a letter from home — another from Carroll. It has been about four months since I last heard from him. He really can write a letter when he decide to. He askt why I hadn't written to him and he of course gave me a little of his good advice. He hardly said I shouldn't had joined the

(over)

navy and if I had stayed on the outside would had be a pretty good job by now. The letter was something a dandy. All I think he wanted to do was to make me feel bad and you know yourself he couldn't do that by his-self.

Jo I'm glad all of you had a nice Christmas this year. Of course I knew you would get lots of presents. I sorry yet you are pine going muddle about the present you going to get from me. Well you will have to stay of them for a while for I wouldn't tell you what it was. Anyway I think you would like to get one — if you can guess what it is — and I'm getting it as a birthday & Christmas together. Please don't have any more sister over it because I think something you like real well.

Hope you & the family are well the colds by now. I sorry about the tobacco bringing a low price this year, Dad said in his letter that they were just about taking it all this year. So I can you think you will be happy before long. Well I hope that you will I bet he really a nice man too.

[[Joan asked if we're going to Australia. Well I don't know they might send us anywhere in the next three months.]]

Joan, there isn't anything new going on out here that you don't know about. In fact we had been out to sea all week firing, but we go back in Pearl Harbor early in the morning.

Please don't be so long about writing next time, Joan. Your letters sure come in handy for me. I look forward every week for a letter from you. I'm still on what so [strikethrough] for some funny one and will stay that. Give my love to all,

                                                    With Lots of Love to You,
                                                                          (Gene)
